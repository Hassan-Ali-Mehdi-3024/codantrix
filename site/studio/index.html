01 Operating principles

Five rules. We won't break them for you.

P.01

We end in a deploy, not a slide deck.

Every engagement closes with running code in your infra or ours, a Loom walkthrough, and a runbook. No "phase 2 proposal" handshake.

P.02

Fixed scope. Fixed price. Fixed timeline.

Written SOW before any work begins. No hourly billing, no surprise invoices. Change requests get their own scoped SOW.

P.03

Code is yours from day one.

We work in your repo. IP transfers on final payment. We don't lock you into a "platform" you have to keep paying us for.

P.04

Plan once. Ship once.

Push back during scoping — we welcome it. Once the SOW is signed and we're building, the only way back is a new system. That's not aggression. That's how we stay fast.

P.05

Small on purpose.

One founder, contractors on demand, no project managers between you and the engineer writing your code. Three engagements running concurrently, max.

02 · How we work

Map first, then build the spine, then wire the agents, then ship.

Every engagement runs on the same four phases: Map, Build, Wire, Ship. Two-day discovery, then orchestration goes in first, then domain agents land on top of the spine, then production deploy with eval gating and a 30-day support tail.

Stateless before stateful. Deterministic before probabilistic. Every agent has a fail mode and a rollback path before it sees real traffic.

Daily async updates inside a shared channel. One weekly sync on camera. Weekly demo videos when there's something to show. No standups for the sake of standups.

03 · Where we are

Lahore, Pakistan. GMT+5. Working US and EU windows.

We're based in Lahore but most of our clients aren't. The studio operates on overlapping US and EU business hours by default — you brief at the end of your day, the build moves overnight, code is on your desk the next morning.

Lahore is GMT+5. That's an 8 — 11 hour offset from US time zones and 4 — 5 hours from European. We've built the workflow around it. The founder bio goes deeper on why we work this way.

04 What we don't do

Anti-services. Honest about scope.

We don't accept hourly billing.

Hours-based work makes everyone slower. Fixed scope keeps the incentive on shipping, not on extending.

We don't drag a one-month build into six.

If we say a system takes 6 weeks, it takes 6 weeks. Padding scope to bill for the time is not how we operate.

We don't take work that fits inside a no-code platform.

If your workflow runs in Lindy or Relevance, hire them — they'll land you faster than we will. We'll tell you on the scope call.

We don't hide behind "discovery phases" or "workshops."

The 30-min scope call is the discovery. By the end you'll know what we'd build, in what order, what it costs, and whether we're the right fit.

We don't build chatbots disguised as products.

Wrapping an LLM in a chat box isn't a product. Multi-agent architecture with orchestration, memory, evals, and a real surface — that's what we build.

We don't take SOC 2 / HIPAA deployments.

Compliance-attested deployments aren't our lane yet. We'll point you to studios that specialize in them.
